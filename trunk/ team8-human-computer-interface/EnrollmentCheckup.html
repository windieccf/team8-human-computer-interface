Important Informations

About Pre-Admission Medical Screening

All Undergraduates and Postgraduates matriculating are required to complete their medical examination before entering the university. The screening will be performed at the University Health Centre.

Candidates who accepted the offer from Medicine, Dentistry or Nursing must also be screened for immunity to Hepatitis B, Chickenpox and Rubella. .

Opening Hours

The Pre-Admission Medical Screening service will be available on Tue/Wed/Thu, 9am - 11.30am / 1.30pm to 5pm

What You Need to Prepare

Required Items

Please bring the following items:

  1. Medical Examination Form
    Please fill in the NUS Admission Medical Examination Form before coming for medical screening at UHC.
  2. Corrective Lenses
    If you wear spectacles or contact lenses, please bring them on the day of your medical examination.
  3. Medical Reports
    For those with pre-existing medical conditions, please bring the relevant medical reports and supporting documents.
Restrictions

Please note the following restrictions:

  1. For female candidates
    Please come for medical examination at least 5 to 7 days after the end of your menses. This is because menstruation will affect the results of the urine test.
  2. Attire
    You will be undergoing a full physical examination and a chest X-ray at UHC, so please come dressed simply and appropriately.
    To ensure that objects that obstruct proper reading of the X-ray films are removed when the chest X-ray is taken, no necklace or nipple rings are allowed during the X-ray examination.
    For this same reason, we advise ladies to wear a PLAIN blouse or T-shirt on the day of examination. In particular, the shirt or blouse should not have any of the following:
    • buttons
    • logos or designs that are printed or embroidered
    • pockets
    • any collar on the shirt/blouse
    You will need to purchase a disposable x-ray gown at a nominal fee if your attire is found to be unsuitable for the X-ray examination.
  3. Fasting
    Fasting is NOT required for the medical examination.
Exemptions

Graduate students (excluding those enrolled in medicine, nursing and dentistry and courses that require additional health requirements) who are NUS staff members or are being employed in local companies may be exempted from the pre-admission medical examination if they have done a SIMILAR medical check-up not more than six months before the time of registration. University Health Centre shall review the exemption on a case by case basis and reserve the right to refuse an exemption.

The Screening Process

The entire process should take between 30 to 90 minutes, depending on queues, and consists of the following steps.

1. Payment

Please present the completed forms and make payment at the payment counter. Please keep the receipt.

The Medical Examination Fees are as follows:

Standard Medical Examination (includes Chest X-Ray) $35.00
Medical Examination without Chest X-Ray $25.00
Standard Medical Examination with Student Pass Examination $50.00
Standard Medical Examination with Hepatitis B, Chickenpox & Rubella Screening $122.00
Standard Medical Examination with Student Pass Examination, Hepatitis B, Chickenpox & Rubella Screening $137.00
* Prices are subjected to prevailing GST and change without prior notice
2. Parameters

Your height, weight, eyesight and colour vision will be checked.

3. Blood Test

It is not required for everyone to go for a blood test for the medical examination.
Blood tests are needed only for:

  1. Student Pass Medical Examinations
  2. Medicine, Dentistry and Nursing Students (Hepatitis B, Chickenpox and Rubella Screening)

If you require a blood test, please fill up Name/NRIC/PP/FIN on Blood Test form and tubes.
After doing so, proceed to treatment room and leave the form in the tray at the door.

4. Blood Pressure

Your blood pressure will be measured.

5. Urine Test

It is required for everyone to do a urine test.
Please inform the staff if you have had your menses within the past 5 days.
Please drink plain water (no sugar) before coming for your medical examination.

6. Medical Review by the Physician

Medical Review by the Physician

7. Chest X-Ray

Bring the X-ray Form with you to the X-ray room.

After the Screening

For Local Students

Collection of NUS Medical Examination Report is not necessary as University Health Centre will be the collection centre. We will inform your School or Faculty about your medical result.

For International Students

Please note the following restrictions:

Exemptions

The Student Pass Medical Report, chest x-ray and blood test results must be collected personally. The reports are available for collection from UHC one week after testing. Please bring along your passport when collecting your reports.
You are required to prepare an authorization letter if you are sending someone to collect your reports on your behalf.
